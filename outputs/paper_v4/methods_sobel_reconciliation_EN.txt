3.4  Outcome (Y) Operationalization and a Full 2×2 Y×M Decomposition

Our primary mediation pipeline reports a cluster-robust z = -15.69
for the JP indirect effect (indirect = -0.0289, 95% CI
[-0.0327, -0.0254]), with the outcome (Y)
operationalized as each agent's round-3 reported probability (a
continuous, agent-level variable, M = stance_direction_r3). A follow-up
robustness analysis returned z = -9.03 (95% CI
[-0.1103, -0.0707])
under Y = converged (binary, session-shared) and M = stance_openness.

To isolate the driver of this gap, we conducted a full 2×2 Y×M
decomposition (supplementary_fix_v3.py, N_boot = 5,000 per cell):

  Y = probability | M = stance_direction_r3   →  z = -15.69
  Y = probability | M = stance_openness        →  z = -17.71
  Y = converged   | M = stance_direction_r3   →  z = -8.93
  Y = converged   | M = stance_openness        →  z = -9.03

Switching M while holding Y = probability produces Δz = 2.02;
switching Y while holding M = stance_direction_r3 produces Δz =
6.76. Y operationalization is therefore the dominant driver
of the reported gap. Note that under Y = probability, stance_openness
(continuous) yields a modestly larger |z| than stance_direction_r3
(discrete) due to greater information content in the b-path; however,
this Δz of 2.02 is negligible relative to the 6.76-unit
shift induced by the Y change.

The mechanism is straightforward. The binary convergence indicator
(Y = converged) is a session-level attribute: all four agents within a
session share the identical value, yielding ICC = 1.000 and an
effective sample size of approximately 1,000 (one per session).
The round-3 probability (Y = probability) varies across agents within a
session (ICC = 0.103), yielding a smaller design effect
(1.31 vs. 4.00) and N_eff ≈ 3,057.
Both pipelines apply cluster-robust standard errors correctly on sim_id;
the difference in precision arises from which dependent variable is
being predicted.

Because ICC = 1.000 for Y = converged implies that agent-level
regressions on this outcome carry no within-session information for the
b-path (M→Y), we additionally report a session-level gold-standard
analysis (n = 1,000 sessions, M = session-mean stance openness):
indirect = -0.06213, z = -4.899, 95% CI
[-0.0879, -0.0388]. This ICC-corrected estimate
corroborates the negative direction and statistical significance
(p < .001) of the mediation pathway.

Both Y-specifications agree in sign, in statistical significance
(p < .001), and in substantive interpretation: JP nationality
attribution is associated with reduced stance flexibility, which in turn
suppresses convergence. We therefore report the continuous-probability
specification as the primary H3 statistic and the session-level
binary-convergence specification as a robustness check.

As an additional, specification-independent check, a session-level
permutation placebo test (N = 100,000 permutations) found the observed
convergence-rate gap exceeded the permuted distribution in only
0.016% of draws (p = 0.00016).

Reporting Convention.

We report z = -15.69 (indirect = -0.0289, 95% CI
[-0.0327, -0.0254], PM = 35.2%) as the
primary H3 statistic (Y = probability, M = stance_direction_r3).
The session-level ICC-corrected estimate (z = -4.899) and
the robustness check under Y = converged agent-level
(z = -9.03) are reported as corroborating evidence,
not as unresolved discrepancies.
